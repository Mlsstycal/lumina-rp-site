Getting Started — Clocking In

Before you can respond to calls or appear on dispatch you must clock in. You have two options:

Option 1 — Duty Sign: Head to the duty sign at the hospital and press E to sign on duty.
Option 2 — F5 Job Menu: Press F5 to open your job menu and toggle on duty from there.

Once on duty you will appear on the map for other medics and dispatch will track your location in real time.

Standard Response Workflow

Follow this workflow every time you respond to a call.

#Action
1Receive dispatch call — acknowledge on radio channel 35
2Respond to scene — use lights and sirens appropriately
3Assess the scene — ensure it is safe before approaching
4Check patient status — radial menu → Ambulance → Check Status
5Treat wounds if needed — radial menu → Ambulance → Heal Player
6Revive patient if downed — radial menu → Ambulance → Revive Player
7Transport to hospital if needed
8Document the call in MDT — create a medical incident report
9Clear the call on radio and return to patrol

Scene Safety Guidelines

Important: EMS is not law enforcement. If a scene is not safe do not enter. Request police backup and stage nearby until the scene is secured.
SituationWhat to do
Active shooter on sceneStage at a safe distance — wait for police to secure before entering
Aggressive patientRequest police backup before treating
Multiple casualtiesTriage — treat most critical first — call for backup medics
Unknown cause of injuryAssess for ongoing danger before approaching patient
Officer down callRespond immediately — highest priority call
//

Do's and Don'ts

✓ Always
  • Clock in before starting your shift
  • Acknowledge dispatch calls on radio
  • Assess scene safety before approaching
  • Document all patient care in the MDT
  • Have firstaid and bandages stocked before patrol
  • Request police backup when scene is unsafe
  • Treat all patients with professionalism and respect
✗ Never
  • Enter an unsafe scene without police clearing it first
  • Refuse to treat a patient without valid RP reason
  • Share patient information out of character
  • Use your ambulance for non-medical purposes
  • Ignore a dispatch call without valid reason
  • Act on information you would not know in character
  • Abuse the revive system or treat it as a game mechanic
